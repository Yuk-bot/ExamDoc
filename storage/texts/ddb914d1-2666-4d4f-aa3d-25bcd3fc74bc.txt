Case Study: Resource Allocation in Cloud Computing Using Optimization Algorithms
Background
TechStream Solutions is a mid-sized cloud service provider serving 500+ enterprise clients
across e-commerce, healthcare, and fintech sectors. The company operates three data centers
with 10,000 virtual machines (VMs) distributed across multiple availability zones.
In Q3 2024, TechStream faced critical challenges:
• Resource wastage: 35% of allocated resources remained underutilized
• Performance issues: 23% of client applications experienced latency > 200ms
• Cost overruns: Monthly infrastructure costs exceeded budget by $450,000
• Customer complaints: 15% increase in support tickets related to performance
The company’s traditional static resource allocation approach couldn't handle the dynamic
workload patterns of modern applications.
The Economic Problem
Core Issues
1. Scarcity of Resources
• Limited physical infrastructure (CPU cores, memory, storage, bandwidth)
• High costs of capacity expansion ($2M+ per data center upgrade)
• Energy consumption constraints (peak load: 5MW per data center)
2. Unlimited Demand
• Clients demand guaranteed performance and availability
• Workload patterns vary significantly (e-commerce peaks during sales, healthcare
steady-state)
• Unpredictable traffic spikes (3x-10x normal load during events)
3. Opportunity Cost
• Over-provisioning to one client means resources unavailable for others
• Idle resources represent lost revenue opportunities
• Investment in infrastructure vs. investment in service innovation
Technical Scenario
Current State Architecture
Infrastructure Inventory:
• Data Center A: 4,000 VMs (40% utilization)
• Data Center B: 3,500 VMs (55% utilization)
• Data Center C: 2,500 VMs (30% utilization)
Client Portfolio (Sample):
Client Industry VMs Allocated Actual Usage Peak Hours SLA Requirement
ShopFast E-commerce 500 60% 6PM-10PM 99.9% uptime
MediCare Healthcare 300 85% 24x7 99.95% uptime
FinPay Fintech 400 70% 9AM-6PM 99.99% uptime
StreamMax Media 350 40% 7PM-12AM 99.5% uptime
Cost Structure:
• VM operational cost: $0.08/hour per VM
• Energy cost: $0.12/kWh
• Over-provisioning penalty: Wasted capacity costs
• Under-provisioning penalty: SLA violations ($1,000-$10,000 per incident)
