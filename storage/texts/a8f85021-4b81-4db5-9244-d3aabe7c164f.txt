X. CONCLUSION
Inthisprojectdifferent16-bitcarryadderdesignshavebeen
tested and compared using a real hardware implementation
on an FPGA board. The results obtained suggest that while
simpledesignslikeRCAandCLAdoexhibitlesscomplexity,
they do also possess greater latency than the parallel Kogge-
Stone Adder design. The FPG A-optimized design performs
competitivelyandithasbeenconcludedthatafullyoptimized
FPGA adder may be the most appropriate when speed and a
compromise in resource usage are priorities.
FUTUREWORK
Other parallel prefix adders such as the Brent-Kung and
Sklansky adder can be implemented to further compare the
performance of the implementations on FPGA, and the power
consumption of each adder design could be investigated.
Further attempts to optimize the Kogge-Stone adder would
result in reduced resource usage on the FPGA board and po-
tentially more competitive performance against the optimized
behavioral design. Additionally, optimization techniques and
pipeliningcanbeexploredtoimproveperformanceandreduce
hardware utilization on FPGA.
